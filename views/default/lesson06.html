The Civil Rights Act of 1964 makes it illegal to retaliate against a person because the person complained about discrimination, filed a charge of discrimination, or participated in an employment discrimination investigation or lawsuit. Q: Have you ever experienced retaliation for speaking out against discrimination?

In other words, your employer cannot take an ""adverse action"" against you for trying to prevent discriminatory actions.
